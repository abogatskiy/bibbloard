# Billboard chart Size Weeks H-H Peak H-H Int H-H

Bibbloard ranks Billboard artists by their chart h-index, inspired by the academic h-index used to measure a scientist's research output. The idea: a single smash hit doesn't make a legend — you need many charting songs, each one holding up on its own.

Weeks h-index: sort your songs by weeks on chart, best to worst. Your h-index is the largest h such that at least h of your songs spent at least h weeks on chart. Peak h-index: same idea, but rank songs by peak distance from the end of the chart (= chart size − peak position), so a Hot 100 #1 scores 99 and #50 scores 50. Integrated h-index: rank songs by their peak-equivalent weeks — each charting week contributes (chart size − position) / chart size, so #1 ≈ 1.0/week and #50 on a 100-chart = 0.5/week. A #1 for 40 weeks scores ≈ 40; a song that barely scraped the bottom scores near zero. Rewards both peak height and longevity, normalised for chart size changes over time.

The h-h-index summarises an entire chart: it's the largest h such that h artists each have h-index ≥ h. Click any row to explore that chart.

Use the Since dropdown for cross-chart comparisons — pick a specific year, since "All time" favours charts with longer histories.
