About this tool

What is the chart h-index?

By analogy with the academic h-index, an artist's chart h-index rewards both breadth and staying power. A single smash hit can't carry you — you need many charting songs, and each one has to hold up.

Weeks h-index: sort your songs by weeks-on-chart, best to worst. Your h-index is the largest h such that at least h of your songs spent at least h weeks on chart.

Peak h-index: same idea, but rank by chart score (= chart size − peak position). A #1 on the Hot 100 scores 99; #50 scores 50. Your peak h-index is the largest h where h songs each scored ≥ h.

What is the h-h-index?

The h-h-index of a chart is the h-index of all the individual h-indices — the largest h such that at least h artists each have h-index ≥ h. It measures how deep the chart's artist pool runs overall.
